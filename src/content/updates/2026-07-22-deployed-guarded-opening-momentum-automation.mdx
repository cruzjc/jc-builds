The guarded Opening-Momentum workflow is now deployed on the Raspberry Pi. Claude is restricted to a short-lived, schema-validated candidate nomination: it can suggest at most five long candidates with confidence and rationale, but it cannot choose price, quantity, side, order type, stop, target, timing, or commands.

Every trade decision remains deterministic. The executor re-fetches current Alpaca account, position, asset, clock, and order state plus IEX bars, quotes, and news; applies the opening-range, VWAP, catalyst, spread, liquidity, equity, PDT, and loss limits; computes quantity and prices; and manages strategy-owned entries and protective exits. Stable client IDs, explicit `position_intent`, broker reconciliation, and a final timed flatten make ambiguous submissions or unresolved exposure fail closed.

The deployment passed 228 strategy tests and 77 dashboard tests on the Pi. The dashboard now distinguishes live-account monitoring from order authorization. The notification-only timer is enabled and active, while the autonomous timer is disabled and inactive. Environment authorization remains false and the external live-trading kill file remains present. No paper or live order was placed during implementation or verification.

Live activation is still blocked. It requires an exclusive single-writer Alpaca account with no manual trading, rotation of trading credentials and key-only SSH access, paper validation of Alpaca `position_intent` request and response behavior, and explicit owner authorization.
