Ghost Lab is now live as a separate screen-first experiment alongside the
existing Safe, Balanced, and Stealth targets. It does not weaken those modes.
Instead, it searches M, Q, and H correction across all eight masks, combines
quarter-turn matrix orientation with geometric angles from -20 to +20 degrees,
and explores placements from 22% to 88% of the artwork's short side.

Candidate ranking now considers local texture, edge alignment, saliency, finder
contrast, and tonal alteration rather than treating average luminance change as
the complete camouflage signal. Passing runs can expose three already-validated
choices: Most hidden, Best balance, and Most reliable. Switching between them
updates the preview and full-resolution export recipe without another search.

The reliability boundary remains explicit. Ghost Lab must decode the exact link
in pristine artwork with both jsQR and ZXing, then pass at least seven of twelve
stress variants independently in each decoder. Download stays disabled when the
gate is missed, and the interface labels Ghost as experimental and screen-first.

On the 640 by 800 garden-lantern sample, the selected Ghost result improved the
internal visual-camouflage estimate from 60.2% to 66.9%, reduced finder contrast
from 50.9% to 42.5%, and reduced average luminance shift from 16.3% to 13.6%
versus Stealth in the same deterministic harness. The weakest decoder passed
seven of twelve variants. These are local regression measurements, not a
universal phone-camera or print guarantee.

All placement analysis, rendering, decoder checks, and export remain on-device.
No cloud image service, database, object storage, or new infrastructure was
introduced in this release.
