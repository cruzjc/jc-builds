Rotated the live Alpaca credentials and verified that the previous key is revoked. The replacement credentials are isolated to the required Raspberry Pi services. Key-only SSH remains available, while password and keyboard-interactive SSH access are disabled.

Competing legacy order writers and schedules are disabled or masked. The existing long was adopted into Opening-Momentum strategy ownership without submitting or changing a broker order, so deterministic protective exits, the timed flatten, and the durable operator-requested full-close path can manage it after activation.

The paper-only certification path now enforces the canonical Alpaca paper endpoint and separate credentials before any broker call. It uses explicit one-share open and full-close intents, stable client IDs, durable recovery for ambiguous submissions, bounded cleanup for simulated partial fills, account-identity binding, and final broker-flat verification. Alpaca requests do not follow redirects or inherit ambient proxy settings.

Deployment verification passed 402 tests on the Pi. A read-only broker check showed the adopted long and zero open orders. No paper or live order was submitted during this update.

Live execution remains hard locked. The autonomous-entry and operator-exit timers are disabled and inactive, both live-action authorizations remain `0`, and the kill file is present. The project remains `ready_but_blocked` until the separate paper key is created, the authorized one-share paper round trip succeeds during a regular session, and the remaining account-security prerequisite is confirmed.
