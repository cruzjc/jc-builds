Seamless QR's second public version replaces its fixed light-and-dark overlay
with a standards-compatible adaptive optimizer. Pixels are changed in only the
direction needed to cross a local camera threshold, and already-correct pixels
remain untouched. The search now evaluates masks, Q/H correction, quarter-turn
rotation, multiple placements and sizes, and all six signal levels.

Validation is stricter as well: jsQR and ZXing must both discover the exact link
inside the complete artwork, and each decoder must independently meet the
selected twelve-variant stress target. Safe, Balanced, and Stealth are now
presented as 95%, 85%, and 70% reliability targets rather than opacity labels.

The result report separates scan robustness from a clearly labeled tonal-change
estimate. Full-resolution export replays the exact winning mask, placement,
rotation, and signal recipe instead of silently running a different search. The
rebuild remains entirely local and is live on the existing public-beta URL.
