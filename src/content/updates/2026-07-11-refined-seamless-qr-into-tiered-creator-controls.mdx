Seamless QR now presents its local creator as two clear tiers. Classic combines
the former Safe, Balanced, and Stealth choices into one continuous transparency
control. Users can position and size the QR region, rotate it directly, and use
a placement preview to understand its footprint before generation.

Ghost Lab remains the medium-tier local optimizer. Based on successful testing
with a real phone camera, its stress threshold was relaxed from seven to six of
twelve variants independently in each decoder, allowing quieter candidates to
survive the search. The non-negotiable gate remains intact: the pristine artwork
must still decode to the exact destination in both jsQR and ZXing.

The creator also clarifies how blending works. The base artwork and QR-driven
correction are treated as separate working layers while the recipe is adjusted;
the downloaded PNG is a single flattened, metadata-stripped image rather than
an editable layered document. Both Classic and Ghost continue to process the
picture entirely on-device.

Research now outlines a possible third, opt-in Generative Lab tier: semantic
best-fit placement, QR-conditioned inpainting or image regeneration,
degradation-aware optimization, and independent real-decoder validation on a
cloud GPU. That is a roadmap, not shipped infrastructure. No cloud upload path,
GPU instance, object bucket, queue, or related AWS or GCP resource was created
for this release.
