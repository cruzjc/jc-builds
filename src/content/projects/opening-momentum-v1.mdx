## Objective

Opening-Momentum v1 turns early-session US-equity data into bounded trade decisions. Claude can nominate a short list of symbols, but Alpaca/IEX market and account data plus fixed risk rules independently determine whether a setup qualifies, calculate size and prices, and control the order lifecycle. The Raspberry Pi deployment includes the renamed Trade Operations dashboard on its HTTPS `/trade/` route, strategy-owned management of the existing long, and a single deterministic order path whose live actions remain behind independent safety locks.

## Strategy boundaries

- New entries are long-only and require the configured opening-range, VWAP, catalyst, spread, and liquidity conditions.
- Incomplete or missing opening-range data is non-actionable; the engine does not infer a trade plan from partial inputs.
- Failed-gap and opening-range failure logic protects only long positions owned by this strategy; it does not open short positions or sell unrelated holdings.
- Alpaca/IEX is the decision-time source of truth for bars, quotes, account state, positions, and market context.
- Claude may nominate at most five same-session `long_candidate` symbols. Its unattended runner exposes only web search and fetch tools; local shell, file, browser, persistence, skill, and configured MCP surfaces are disabled. Price, quantity, side, stop, target, order type, timing, command, and unknown fields are rejected.
- A new entry requires a fresh observed breakout, executable ask within a deterministic limit, current catalyst and liquidity checks, PDT and live-equity policy, a one-entry-per-session cap, and a maximum proposed notional of $50.
- Entries use whole-share DAY limit brackets after the breakout is observed. Protective exits cancel related strategy orders, re-read the live remaining long quantity, and use a risk-reducing DAY market sell. Explicit Alpaca `position_intent`, stable per-session client IDs, durable broker evidence, and reconciliation prevent blind retry.
- Trade Operations separates strategy-owned and external positions, recent automated-order activity, and recent manual-close activity. Its durable full-close workflow retains mutually exclusive strategy and external-position modes so an operator cannot apply both paths to the same action.

## Current state

The live Alpaca credentials were rotated and isolated to the required Pi services; the prior key was verified revoked. Key-only SSH remains available, while password and keyboard-interactive SSH are disabled. Competing legacy order writers and schedules are disabled or masked, leaving the guarded Opening-Momentum executor as the intended single writer.

The existing long position was adopted into strategy ownership without submitting or modifying a broker order. Deterministic failed-gap protection, the timed flatten, and the durable operator-requested full-close path can manage it after activation. Trade Operations labels the position and recent activity according to that ownership model while keeping direct broker mutation disabled.

The paper-only certification path now requires the canonical paper endpoint, separate paper credentials, explicit confirmation, stable client IDs, durable ambiguous-submission recovery, bounded partial-fill cleanup, and final broker-flat verification. Redirect and ambient-proxy credential paths are disabled. The deployed Pi passed 402 tests after installation, and a read-only broker snapshot showed the adopted long with zero open orders.

Live execution remains hard locked: the autonomous-entry and operator-exit timers are disabled and inactive, both live-action authorizations are `0`, and the kill file is present. Operational status remains `ready_but_blocked` until the separate paper key is created, the authorized one-share paper buy and full close succeeds during a regular session, and the remaining account-security prerequisite is confirmed.
