## Objective

Help people in Hawaiʻi choose a nearby fuel stop by the cost of the purchase and
the drive, rather than by pump price or distance alone. The experience is
mobile-first and deliberately asks passengers or parked drivers to operate it.

## How it works

One browser location reading starts a server-side HERE Fuel Prices search.
HERE Matrix Routing adds road distance and ETA for up to 15 stations, while a
collapsed static HERE map shows the numbered top ten only after the user asks
for it.

The list can be sorted by Best Value, Lowest Price, or Closest. Best Value keeps
the provider price and route inputs visible and labels the app's calculation
separately:

```text
trip-adjusted total = gallons × station price
  + (one-way road miles ÷ MPG) × median fresh local price
```

Regular, midgrade, premium, and diesel are selectable when HERE supplies a
compatible price. The estimate defaults to 10 gallons and 25 MPG, with quick
presets for changing both assumptions.

## Current state

The public Hawaiʻi pilot is live at
[where-get-gas.disphius.chatgpt.site](https://where-get-gas.disphius.chatgpt.site/).
Its launch smoke test returned 15 distinct central-Honolulu stations with
Regular USD-per-gallon prices reported within 24 hours and 15 usable road
routes.

There are no accounts, advertising analytics, saved coordinates, or location
history. Each result now offers a one-tap anonymous accuracy report. The stored
report contains the station name and identifier, fuel grade, accuracy choice,
and time—but no coordinates, free text, or persistent user or device ID.

Only fuel, MPG, gallons, and navigation preferences may stay on the current
device. Prices can still change before arrival, so the site keeps update age
visible and asks users to verify the pump.
